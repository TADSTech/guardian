# Guardian product brief

## Promise

**Understand. Stay safe. You're not alone.**

Guardian helps people make sense of digital messages and everyday information through WhatsApp. A reply should answer three things: what this is, whether it is risky, and what to do next.

## Primary users

- Older adults who need larger text and patient, plain-language guidance.
- People with low literacy who benefit from voice interaction and short explanations.
- People who need less clutter and more clarity when assessing a message or document.

## First release journeys

1. Forward a suspicious message or link → receive a clear risk level, reasons, and next steps.
2. Send a prescription or document photo → receive a plain-language explanation with medical-safety boundaries.
3. Send a voice note or ask a question by voice → receive a voice or text response.
4. Send a website link → receive a simplified, accessibility-aware summary and a safety assessment.

## Safety language

- Never imply that Guardian has confirmed a scam unless the evidence supports it.
- Never ask users to share an OTP, password, PIN, or banking information.
- Healthcare explanations must state that they are informational and direct users to a qualified clinician for decisions.
- When harm is plausible or information is incomplete, prefer a protective next step over a polished answer.
